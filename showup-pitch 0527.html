Every show tracks source (manual vs Living Room), Influenced By (which friend prompted the add) and Added At timestamp. Every reaction captures sentiment and timestamp. Session count and lastActive on every user. All invisible, all feeding the influence graph from day one.

Full settings screen with room theme selector, profile editing and legal links. Standalone privacy policy at /privacy.html ready for attorney review. Consent line on onboarding screen. Terms of Use placeholder in place.

Competitive Landscape
Why existing apps fall short.
ShowUp does not compete by having the most features or the deepest metadata. It competes by being easier, warmer, more social and more human than anything currently available. The product should never feel like homework.
📽

✨
ShowUp
Wins through simplicity, human recommendations and friend-focused discovery. Warm UX. Mainstream usability. Low-friction engagement. Social-first from the first screen to the last.

The App
Four screens. One living room.
ShowUp is deliberately simple. Four tabs, one social hub, one settings screen. Every feature earns its place.
📺
Currently Watching on My TV
Add shows you're actively watching with their streaming service. When you finish, hit Done Watching ✓ — the show moves to Already Binged with a confetti celebration. Shows grouped by streaming service.
🍿
My List
Save your next binge. Add shows you want to watch with their streaming service. Queue It Up moves a show straight to watching. Grouped by service so you can see everything on each platform at a glance.
📼
Already Binged
Your finished shows. Rate each 1 to 5 remotes. Leave a comment — "What did you think?" The more you rate the richer your data and the more valuable Friends Favorites rankings become for everyone.
🏠
The Living Room
Your social hub. See Your Crowd's watching lists, tap into any friend's profile, leave reactions. Toggle to Friends Favorites for crowd-sourced rankings — every show rated, averaged and ranked with who rated what.
👤

Name, city, age range, gender. Editable anytime. Private data never shown to other users. Feeds into future personalized recommendations and psychographic matching.

The Business
How ShowUp makes money on the way to 100,000+ users.
Three revenue paths in priority order — starting with the easiest and building toward the most valuable.
Priority #1 — Start Now
Affiliate Partnerships
ShowUp users are actively deciding what to watch and where. Every time a user signs up for a new streaming service after seeing a show on ShowUp — we earn a commission. Netflix, Hulu, HBO Max, Paramount+ and others all have affiliate programs. Most natural revenue model for a show tracking app. Zero user friction.
$5–25 per new subscriber referred.
Priority #2 — Build Relationships Now
Brand Partnerships
ShowUp's living room positioning is a natural fit for wine brands, spirits companies, snack brands and streaming services launching new shows. Sponsored show recommendations, branded Living Room moments, curated watch lists from partners. Premium placement for brands that match the audience's lifestyle.
$1,000–50,000 per campaign. Start conversations at 1,000+ users.
Priority #3 — The Long Game
Data Licensing
The primary exit and revenue strategy. Anonymized, aggregated psychographic and behavioral data sold to streaming services, advertisers, content producers and data firms. ShowUp knows not just what people watch but who they are, how they live and who influenced their viewing choices. That combination doesn't exist anywhere else.
Serious buyer conversations at 100,000+ users. Acquisition range at >100,000 engaged users with complete profiles.
Target exit: Data firm acquisition. Companies like Nielsen, Comscore, Antenna and Parrot Analytics pay significant money for exactly what ShowUp is building — behavioral and psychographic data tied to entertainment consumption with a verified social influence layer. Realistic acquisition range: $2M–$20M depending on user scale and data quality at time of sale. Timeline: 12+ months post-launch with strong growth.

The goal
The Moat
What no one else can copy.
Pure show tracking apps are commodities. Letterboxd does it for movies. Goodreads does it for books. The TV space has no clear winner — and none of them have what ShowUp is quietly building underneath. This is not a feature. It is a structural advantage that compounds over time.
🔶
The Influence Graph
ShowUp silently tracks when a show spreads from one friend to another — who saw it in a Living Room, who added it next, whose recommendations actually get followed. Nielsen has been trying to build this for 20 years through surveys and panels. ShowUp builds it organically through a product people love using every day.
🫎
Psychographic + Behavioral
ShowUp knows what you watch AND who you are. Career, hobbies, household type, viewing habits — correlated with specific show ratings and friend influence. That combination does not exist anywhere else. Streaming services pay significant money to understand their audiences. ShowUp knows more than they do.
🤝
First Party Trust
Users choose to share this data. They fill in their career, their hobbies, their household. That consent and trust — built through a product they genuinely enjoy — is the foundation of a data asset that survives any privacy regulation change. As third-party cookies die, first-party data becomes the most valuable asset in digital media.
🏠
The Living Room Network
ShowUp is not a solo app — it is a network. Every user who joins brings their social graph. Every friend connection creates a new node in the influence map. Networks compound — the value of each new user makes every existing user's data more valuable. This is why scale matters and why timing matters now.

What's Coming
After Supabase — the next phase.
The current prototype validates the concept. Supabase connects real accounts, real data and real friend networks. These features are designed and ready to build immediately after — in rough priority order.
Real user accounts with magic link login
Email-based login with no password. Data saves permanently between sessions. Every user has their own profile, their own lists, their own Living Room. The app becomes a real product, not a prototype.
Real friend connections and permissions
Invite links that actually work. Friend requests with pending and accepted states. Four relationship states enforced: pending (no access), active (full mutual visibility), hidden (mutual invisibility — neither party sees the other and the hidden person is not notified) and removed.
TMDb show search and poster art
Users type a show name and real results appear with artwork. No more free-form text. Titles become standardized — critical for accurate Friends Favorites rankings across the network. Every show card gets real poster art. Single biggest visual upgrade to the app.
Living Room theme saved permanently
Users pick their room and it saves. Room choice becomes a stored data point — lifestyle preference correlated with viewing behavior and psychographic profile.
Influence graph goes live
Source tracking, influencedBy and timestamp fields start populating a real database. The social influence map begins building from day one. Captured automatically — no user action required.
App Store submission
Supabase enables real authentication required by both Apple and Google for store approval. App wrapped with Capacitor and submitted to both stores. Apple Developer Account ($99/year) and Google Play ($25 one-time).
Taste badges — earned through real behavior
Badges displayed on profiles earned from genuine activity: Trendsetter (added a show before any friends), Good Influence (5 friends added a show after seeing it on your list), Binge Master, Top Critic. Gamification that reinforces the core social behavior ShowUp is built on.
"People like you are watching" recommendations
A recommendation engine using psychographic matching — showing users what people with the same career, hobbies and household type are rating highly. ShowUp's killer feature at scale. Requires 50-100 users with complete profiles before the matches mean anything.
AI-assisted show discovery
"I want a gritty crime thriller" — conversational show discovery powered by AI and TMDb data, filtered through what people like you are rating highly on ShowUp. A genuinely differentiated feature that no existing tracking app offers.
Fun profile stats
Hours watched by platform, favorite genres, most used streaming services, top-rated shows — displayed on user profiles. Increases engagement, encourages return visits and gives users a reason to fill in more data.
